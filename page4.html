I Grew Up With My Plants

~~~METHODOLOGY~~~

A narration of the processes and methods I used in raising my plants

I got the samplings from my grandmother last October 3, 2018 which she bought from the market. After she gave them to me, I imeediately replaced their container with small pots so that I can keep them in the house and observe i em, also they'll look cute in it. 
 and also they'll look cute init. I placed them in the rooftop to assure they'll get enough sunlight and then I watered them before I left.

The next morning, I watered them before going to school. After the school hours, I went home and immediately went to where I left them and thought they're fine. But they were not. It lookedlikethey were dehydrated or something. So I watered them and after a few hours, they went back to normal and left it there again. I know it's  wrong, but this became my 1st method in raising them.

After a month, my plants were still in their position, confusingly not growing! Without searching for the cause, I though it was because of the small pots. It's too small for them to grow. And it looked like they were dying. So I thought, it's time to set them free in the wild which was in our grandparent's backyard or what we call nanay's garden. It's just a few blocks away.

My grandmother helped me in planting them because she knows the areas where there's enough sunlight and less pests. After planting them, she said that the soil where we planted them was rich and has a fertilizer in it. I didn't understand first what she meant, but after a week, I saw a huge difference. The plant grew twice their normal size. It's like seeing childrens grow. 

I'm visiting my plant every 2 days after that knowing my grandmother will water it for me if i'm not around, because it's already dark when I reach home after school hours. Sadly, I always forgoting my camera when i'm visiting my plants.  So I can't show updated pictures of my plants.

Problems occured after a few weeks. Pests starts showing and eats the leaves of my plants. My grandmother told me to place the most damaged plant to a pot so that some snails can't reach it. Because we lack of big pots, we let the other plant to stay where it was, near a wall, hiding from the pests.

Sadly, after a week, this final week, the plant I left near the got a bites on it. I still couldn't find a pot so I decided to pot some sticks around it for protection from the pests. 

The plant I placed on a pot still's the same. Has a lot of damaged leaves. My grandmother told me to cut the dead leaves to for it not to spread? I didn't get what she told me but I did it anyway knowing my grandmother knows a lot about planting and taking care of plants. Surprisingly, the plants stayed alive after all the they went through. And maybe this January, It'll start blooming flowers
